PAYOUTS & ODDS

Everything on this page is for the exact pay tables used at these tables. Virtual chips only — no real money. House edge is the average share of a bet the casino keeps over the long run: a 2% edge means that for every 1,000 wagered, you'd expect to end about 20 down after many, many hands. Any single session can of course swing far either way.

HOW EACH BET SETTLES

Ante pays even money when you beat the dealer — but only if the dealer qualifies with a pair or better (82.6% of hands); otherwise it pushes. Blind always stays in action: it pays the table below when you win with a straight or better, pushes on smaller wins, and loses when the dealer wins. Play is your raise — even money, no qualification needed. The earlier you raise, the more you can bet: 4x or 3x before the flop, 2x on the flop, or 1x at the river. Trips looks only at your final hand. Hole Card Bonus is decided the moment you're dealt (it even pays on a fold). Bad Beat Bonus is consolation insurance: it pays when you lose the showdown holding three of a kind or better.
